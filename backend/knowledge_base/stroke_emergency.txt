Title: Stroke — Emergency
Source: unknown
Version: 2025-11-26
Tags: emergency, neurology

Core emergency signs (F.A.S.T.):
- F: Facial droop (one side droops)
- A: Arm weakness (one arm drifts down)
- S: Speech difficulty (slurred or strange speech)
- T: Time — if any sign is present, call emergency services immediately

Other signs:
- Sudden confusion, vision loss, sudden severe headache, sudden difficulty walking or coordination

Immediate action:
- CALL local emergency services immediately and record symptom onset time.
- Do NOT give food, drink, or medications unless told by paramedics.
- Rapid arrival at a stroke-capable hospital enables time-sensitive treatments.

Safety note:
- Emergency guidance only. Call emergency services immediately for suspected stroke.
